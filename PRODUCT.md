# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The builder (a CS student assembling a resume-grade portfolio piece) and anyone
they demo it to: recruiters, interviewers, and the ElevenLabs team (the Step-9
pitch). Users arrive to *make something*: paste a script, direct the
performance, listen, download. Sessions are short, hands-on, and iterative —
tune a line, re-listen, repeat.

## Product Purpose

Cue lets you direct an AI voice the way a director talks to an actor — in plain
English, no sliders. One direction restyles a whole script; speaker labels turn
it into a multi-voice scene; the result stitches into one downloadable
recording. Success = the demo sells itself in a 60-second screen recording, and
every piece is explainable in an interview.

## Brand Personality

Precision workshop. The interface is a **work surface** — a green self-healing
cutting mat — and the user's script is the material laid on it. Three words:
precise, tactile, unhurried. Era cue: 2000s utility software (boxy panels,
title bars, dense mono labels, honest borders) rendered with modern craft. The
amber "cue light" is the one theatrical note: it means *we're live*.

## Anti-references

- Generic dark-mode AI SaaS: glassmorphism, purple gradients, glowing mesh
  heroes, floating cards with soft mega-shadows.
- The previous Cue UI: a single anonymous dark card with default-looking
  inputs ("trash" — the user).
- Flat minimal "empty" design that reads as an unstyled prototype.

## Design Principles

1. **The mat is the room.** One committed surface (the cutting mat) carries the
   identity; panels sit on it like tools, they don't float over it.
2. **Chrome that measures.** Decoration must read as instrumentation — rulers,
   grids, registration marks, counters — never as ornament.
3. **The script is the star.** The user's words get the highest contrast and
   the most space; controls stay quiet until needed.
4. **State is always visible.** Rendering, playing, cached, which brain/engine —
   the console always tells the truth about what's happening.
5. **Familiar controls, distinctive room.** Standard selects, buttons, and
   textareas — the personality lives in the surface and typography, not in
   reinvented affordances.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA contrast (≥4.5:1 body text) on every surface, including on the mat
  green itself.
- All motion (equalizer, cue light, transitions) respects
  `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Grid/ruler decoration is `aria-hidden`; the interface works identically
  without it.
